# Code of Conduct

BitWars is a game, and its community should be a good place to hang out. This
document is the short version of "be a decent human." It applies to everyone —
players, contributors, and maintainers alike.

## The short version

Be respectful. Assume good faith. Help newcomers. Disagree about code, not
about people. We're all here because we like building and playing a chunky
voxel shooter — keep it fun.

## Where this applies

This Code of Conduct applies in all BitWars community spaces:

- The GitHub repository — issues, pull requests, discussions, and code review.
- The [Discord server](https://discord.gg/R9HEJBqJAX).
- Anywhere someone is officially representing the project (e.g. an official
  social account or event).

In short: if you're interacting with the BitWars community, this applies.

## What we expect

- **Be welcoming and patient.** People have different skill levels, languages,
  and time zones. A kind answer is worth more than a clever one.
- **Be respectful of differing opinions** and review feedback. Critique the
  idea or the code, and explain your reasoning.
- **Give and accept constructive feedback gracefully.** A rejected PR or a
  request for changes isn't personal.
- **Own your mistakes,** apologize, and learn from them.
- **Put the community first.** Do what's best for the project and the people
  in it, not just for yourself.

## What's not okay

The following behavior is unacceptable in any BitWars space:

- Insults, demeaning comments, or personal attacks.
- Harassment of any kind, whether in public channels or in private messages.
- Discriminatory language or jokes, including those targeting someone's
  identity, background, or beliefs.
- Unwelcome attention or contact after someone has asked you to stop.
- Sharing someone else's private information (such as a real name, address, or
  contact details) without their explicit permission.
- Deliberately derailing, spamming, or disrupting discussions, the game, or
  other people's contributions.
- In the game itself: cheating, exploiting, or griefing in ways meant to ruin
  the experience for others.
- Anything else a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a friendly,
  collaborative community.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers and moderators are responsible for clarifying these standards and
are expected to take fair, appropriate action in response to behavior they
consider inappropriate, threatening, or unwelcome. They may remove, edit, or
reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that don't
align with this Code of Conduct, and will explain moderation decisions when
it's appropriate to do so.

## Reporting

If you see or experience behavior that breaks this Code of Conduct, report it
**privately** — please don't call it out in a public issue or channel.

- **Discord:** direct-message a moderator or the maintainer
  ([@ItzAmirreza](https://github.com/ItzAmirreza)) in the
  [Discord server](https://discord.gg/R9HEJBqJAX).

All reports are reviewed and handled discreetly. We respect the privacy and
safety of whoever reports an issue. (Note: software security vulnerabilities
go through [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md), not here.)

## How reports are handled

Maintainers follow these rough guidelines when deciding what a violation
deserves. The exact response is always a judgment call based on the situation.

1. **A quiet word.** For a first, minor slip — say, language that was out of
   line — a maintainer will reach out privately to explain why it wasn't okay.
   A public apology may be requested.
2. **A warning.** For a clearer or repeated issue, a formal warning with
   consequences for continued behavior. This may include a temporary break from
   interacting in community spaces.
3. **A temporary ban.** For a serious violation or sustained bad behavior, a
   temporary ban from all community spaces. No public or private contact with
   the people involved during this period.
4. **A permanent ban.** For a pattern of violations, harassment, or aggression
   toward individuals or groups, a permanent ban from the community.
